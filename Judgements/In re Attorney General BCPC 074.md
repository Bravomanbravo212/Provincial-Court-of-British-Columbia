**INTRODUCTION**

[1] On April 20th, 2025 Citizen of the Province of British Columbia JohnTheSquidCatcher filed a mandamus application requesting the Premier of the Province of British Columbia be ordered by the court to comply with the [Executive Agency Order](docs.google.com/document/d/1dAc7aUlw79txHTPBU4DKqy8GfZkfH5kzmamSjkOnIFE/edit) (the "Order")

[2] The Order summarily requires that indviduals within positions of authority are regulated in the positions they may hold simultaneously.

**FACTS OF THE CASE**

[3] At the time of the application being filed, chewalicousJr (the "subject") was sworn in as a [Member of the Legislative Assembly of British Columbia](https://prnt.sc/MR9bEAry49j1)

[4] The subject was simultaneously and at the time of this order being written, is additionally the [Deputy Chief Constable of the Vancouver Police Department](https://media.discordapp.net/attachments/1363585170547937441/1363647195991245030/Screenshot_20250420_200750_Discord.jpg?ex=6806caea&is=6805796a&hm=b712f7514042c318a73845338a6cdb9efe9a4ea2ef2e72eb348e017396be49f4&=&format=webp&width=377&height=807)

**IS THE SUBJECT IN VIOLATION**

[5] The Order requires that "No given individual shall hold more than one of the following offices at the same time [...] Assemblyperson / Speaker of the Assembly, Department 1IC / Department 2IC, [...]"

[6] Therefore it is required that the subject must additionally comply with the remainder of this section "Should this occur, the individual will have 72 hours to resign from all but one (1) of the conflicting positions to become in compliance with this clause."

**JUDGEMENT**

[7] It is in the opinion of this court that the subject of this order is in violation of the Order and need be required to comply.

[8] As such the following orders are made:
- chewalicousJr is required to within a 72 hour period from the release of this judgement:
Resign from all but one (1) position as listed within Section 6 of the Order.

Failure to comply with the above orders will result in penalty of Disobeying a Court Order.
